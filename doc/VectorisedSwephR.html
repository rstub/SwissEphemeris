Some simple steps to do calculations

To compute the position of celestial body or star with SE (Swiss Ephemeris) for two dates (aka using vectorisation), you do the following steps:

  1. First load swephR and constants used in swephR/SE:
library(swephR)
data(SE)
  1. Optionaly set the directory path of the ephemeris files, e.g.:
swe_set_ephe_path("C:\\sweph\\ephe")
  1. For a specific date, compute the Julian day number (in below example: 1 January 2000CE at 12:00 UT (or J2000.0) and 1 January 1BCE at 12:00):
year1 <- 2000
year2 <- 0
month <- 1
day <- 1
hour <- 12
jdut <- vec_julday(c(year1,year2), month, day, hour, SE$GREG_CAL)
#>   year month day hour gregflag
#> 1 2000     1   1   12        1
#> 2    0     1   1   12        1
jdut
#> [[1]]
#> [1] 2451545
#> 
#> [[2]]
#> [1] 1721060
  1. Compute (using Moshier ephemeris) the positions (longitude, latitude, distance, longitude speed and latitude speed) of a planet or other celestial bodies (in below example: the Sun):
ipl <- SE$SUN
iflag <- SE$FLG_MOSEPH + SE$FLG_SPEED
result <- vec_calc_ut(c(jdut[[1]],jdut[[2]]), ipl, iflag)
#>     jd_ut ipl iflag
#> 1 2451545   0   260
#> 2 1721060   0   260
result
